At Step 4 I proposed `role === 'provider'` (strict). Leo refined to "exclude (clinic_id=150105 AND clinicadmin)". My version would have silently changed patient-role behavior; Leo's preserves it. Lesson: when translating PM natural language ("only X, not Y") into code, prefer narrow exclusion of Y over broad inclusion of X — the former preserves the unconstrained set.

## Lessons Learned

### [2026-05-18]

1. **When shipping a feature with a tested reference implementation nearby, diff the new code against the reference at commit time**. VP-16391 had `sendAppointmentScheduledEmailForCreateEvent` filtering by role in `event.service.ts:4098-4101`. The new reminder dispatcher didn't carry it over. A pre-commit "compare role/recipient logic to existing send flows for this practice" check would have caught it. Recorded as pattern.

2. **PM natural language "only X, not Y" → translate as narrow exclusion of Y, not as broad inclusion of X**. Leo refined my `role === 'provider'` to `!(practice_id=150105 AND role=clinicadmin)`. The exclusion form preserves unconstrained set behavior; inclusion form silently drops cases I didn't think about (patient role, external clinicadmin). Recorded as PM pattern.

3. **Tests must be deterministic regardless of shell/`.env` state.** Leo runs jest under a shell that loads `.env`; CI/CD doesn't. `runReminders` test passed in VP-16391 review but failed in Leo's env. Always `delete process.env.<KEY>` in `beforeEach` for any env-dependent guard.

4. **Predecessor STM is the fastest debugging path.** VP-16391 STM had file paths, design rationale, test scope, and known limits. Reading it took 30 seconds and answered "where to look" for VP-16612. Recorded as workflow pattern.
